Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's Remarkable TimeLine.


  • 1931- born in Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954-graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College.
  • 1960-graduated in Aeronautic Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1965-started his work in expandable rocket projext independently at DRDO.
  • 1969-transfered to ISRO where he was project director for India's First Satellite Launch Vehicle.
  • 1974- played a pivotal organisational, technical and political role in India's Pokhran-II.
  • 1981-received India's third civilian award:Padma Bhushan from GOI for his contribution to Nation.
  • 1990-received India's second civilian award:Padma Vibhushan from GOI for his contribution to Nation
  • 1997-received India's first civilian award:Bharat Ratna for his excellent services to Nation.
  • 1998-received Veer Savarkar Award for his dynamic decisions in field of space science from GOI.
  • 2002-became 11th President of India.
  • 2011-was offered with IEEE Honorary Membership.
  • 2015-passed away during a lecture delivering to IIM Shilong students about "Creating a Livable Planet Earth".
